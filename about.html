Our Mission

Monasteries 360° is dedicated to preserving and sharing the rich cultural heritage of Sikkim's Buddhist monasteries through immersive digital experiences. Our mission is to make these sacred spaces accessible to people around the world while promoting cultural understanding and appreciation.

The Spiritual Heritage of Sikkim

Sikkim, nestled in the Eastern Himalayas, is home to numerous ancient Buddhist monasteries that serve as repositories of Tibetan Buddhist culture, art, and traditions. These monasteries, known locally as "gompas," are not just places of worship but also centers of learning, art, and community gathering.

Each monastery has its unique architectural style, religious artifacts, and historical significance. From the ancient Dubdi Monastery established in 1701 to the magnificent Pemayangtse Monastery with its seven-tiered painted wooden structure representing Guru Rinpoche's heavenly abode, Sikkim's monasteries offer glimpses into centuries of Buddhist tradition.

Tourism in Sikkim

Sikkim is a jewel in India's northeastern crown, offering breathtaking landscapes, rich biodiversity, and vibrant cultural experiences. The state's tourism focuses on sustainable practices that preserve its natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Natural Beauty

From the majestic Kanchenjunga, the world's third-highest peak, to pristine lakes, hot springs, and lush forests, Sikkim's landscapes offer unparalleled beauty.

Biodiversity

Sikkim is a biodiversity hotspot with over 4,500 species of flowering plants, 550 species of birds, and numerous rare and endangered wildlife species.

Cultural Heritage

The state's unique blend of Nepali, Bhutia, and Lepcha cultures is reflected in its festivals, cuisine, traditional crafts, and architectural styles.

Adventure Tourism

Sikkim offers numerous opportunities for trekking, river rafting, mountain biking, and other adventure activities amidst stunning natural settings.
